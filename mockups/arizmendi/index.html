Our Story

Baked with care,
owned by everyone.

Forty years ago, Berkeley's now-famous cooperative, the Cheeseboard, opened its doors. In 1997, inspired by their own success, they helped open another bakery based on cooperative principles. Arizmendi Oakland debuted that year on Lakeshore Avenue, named after the Basque labor organizer José Maria Arizmendiarrieta.


The endeavor was a hit, so what next? They decided to open another bakery, this time in San Francisco. In October of 2000, Arizmendi Bakery San Francisco was born. The Cheeseboard's gift to us has become our gift to the neighborhood.

FAQ

Frequently asked.

Why the name "Arizmendi"?
Arizmendi is the shortened name of José Maria Arizmendiarrieta, a young priest who inspired and helped found the Mondragón Cooperatives in the Basque Country of Spain. What started as one firm and roughly 25 people in 1956 is now a major international business with over 34,000 workers in some 100 worker-owned enterprises. The name acknowledges his inspiration in our effort to expand democratically owned and operated workplaces.
How do I bake a lite-baked pizza?
  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Remove pizza from box and place directly on oven rack.
  3. Bake for 5-10 minutes until cheese starts to brown.
How do I make my own pizza using your dough?
  1. With lightly floured hands, round the dough, cover with a damp towel and let rest 1-3 hours depending on kitchen temperature.
  2. Press dough down with pads of fingers and let rest another 10-20 minutes.
  3. Get your pan ready (cookie sheet works fine) — brush with olive oil and dust with cornmeal.
  4. When dough stretches easily and doesn't spring back, stretch to desired size and thickness.
  5. Add toppings!
  6. Bake in pre-heated 500° oven for 10-15 minutes until crust feels firm and toppings are browned.
  7. While baking, heat a griddle on your stove. Transfer pizza from pan to griddle for a golden, crisp crust.
How long will your pizza dough last?
Pizza dough will last 1-3 days in the refrigerator. It can also be frozen for up to two weeks.

Mission Statement

To continue to foster and uphold a sustainable workplace, while making our cooperative a place which can financially support our worker-owners.

To operate in a democratically organized way, ensuring each worker-owner has agency in the co-op. To support our members through teamwork and with respect, and to embrace the value in all individuals that make our bakery a whole.
